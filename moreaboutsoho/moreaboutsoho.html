Significant buildings/structures in the Cast Iron District 479 Broadway: Largest Cast Iron Building in the District 647 Broadway: Known as the Palace, there was an apartment in there that belonged to a man named Dan Mancuso, who opened his apartment to gay men and women to freely express themselves through dance and disco. The Heebie Jeebies: The Eden of Punk Rock. The Ramones were first heard and popularized here. Canal Street: The street used to be a canal connecting the East and Hudson Rivers. The original canal is hidden under the street and branches of it can be accessed through a west side park manhole, as well as through the Fulton Street Subway station. If you are on the Hudson River by boat you can see original canal entrances under certain streets.

Income/Poverty

As of 2018, SoHo’s median household income is $150,600. Due to this, the neighborhood is considered high-income compared to the rest of NYC. Moreover, since the area is costly, it has a lower poverty rate of 6.6%.

Population/Race

About 151,979 people lived in Community District 2, SoHo/Greenwich Village, in 2018. During this time, the majority of SoHo’s population consisted of white, with 72.6% as the percentage. Furthermore, 14.5% of the residents were Asian. With 7.4% identifying as Hispanic, and finally, 1.7% consisting of black.

Education

In 2019, over 80% of Community District 2’s 4th-grade students performed at grade level in both math and English Language Arts. This is significantly higher than the rest of Manhattan and the city, with both only reaching the 50-60% range.

Crime

In 2019, the rate of crime was 26.7 per 1,000 residents. This is notably higher compared to the rate citywide; 11.3 per 1,000 residents. The research found from en.wikipedia.org
